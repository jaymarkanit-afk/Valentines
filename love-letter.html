Dear Trexy ,

I don’t really know how to begin this letter because I don’t know if this letter is going to be an invitation letter, a Valentine’s letter, or something in between. This letter is maybe the hardest part of creating this website because I have to find the right words for it, but I guess there is no such thing as the “right” words — just how you feel, right?

To be honest, I don’t really know what I’m going to write in this letter, but I just know I need to write it. Maybe I’m going to start with how I talked to God before I slept the other night.

I prayed to give you a good rest, and I asked Him to keep you safe. And I prayed that maybe He would allow you to hug and see a child’s smile so that you might feel loved a little bit, or maybe to let you feel a stranger’s warmth, or to give you a beautiful flower on a normal day.

I told God how your presence stays with me even after our conversation ends, and how you turn ordinary moments into something warmer. And honestly, I’m really glad I get to interact with you. I’ve only dreamt of this moment, and now I’m living in it.

And if nothing comes from all of this, I’m already grateful I got to care this softly and this honestly — even just in prayer.

I can still remember the first time I saw you. You looked so lovely in your Sunday outfit, and I realized you are much more good-looking as I got closer to you. You look so nice and have so much warmth in you.

I just wished we had more time to interact with each other personally, and so with this letter, it’s very tragic if I won’t ask to invite you — not just this coming Valentine’s, but also to invite you to go out with me in every chance I could get. Because I think I’ll have more fun with you in person, and I would love to experience each one of them.

But since there is a Valentine’s Day coming, I would like to ask you...

CAN I BE YOUR VALENTINE?
